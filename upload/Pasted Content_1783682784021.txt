## ABSOLUTE CORE RULE

Normal users must never manually create debit/credit journal entries during day-to-day work.

Users only interact with business forms:

- Counter Sale Bill
- Online Sale Bill
- OFC / Out-of-City Sale Bill
- Purchase Bill
- Expense Batch
- Receipt Voucher
- Payment Voucher
- Contra Entry
- Petty Cash
- Journal Voucher — Accountant/Owner only, for manual adjustment

Every business form automatically generates a balanced double-entry voucher through:

- `vouchers`
- `voucher_lines`

Accounting is invisible infrastructure. The user fills a business form; the system posts the accounting.

Double-entry must be enforced at the server/database layer, not only in UI.

Do NOT try to enforce voucher balance using a simple Postgres row-level CHECK constraint, because voucher balancing requires aggregate validation across multiple `voucher_lines`.

Voucher posting must happen only through a server-side transaction/RPC such as `post_voucher()`.

### Accounting Engine

Create:

- `vouchers`
- `voucher_lines`

Every sale, purchase, expense, receipt, payment, contra, journal, opening balance, return, cancel/void, and adjustment creates a voucher.

Voucher posting only through server-side `post_voucher()` RPC.

`post_voucher()` must:

- run in a transaction
- insert voucher header
- insert voucher lines
- validate total debit = total credit
- reject the entire transaction if unbalanced
- write audit log
- return voucher id

Direct client inserts into `voucher_lines` must be blocked by RLS.

Do not allow manually edited balance fields to drift from ledger.

Balance columns may exist only as recalculable/cache fields protected by server-side logic.

#### Invoice Number Rule

Counter Sale, Online Sale, and OFC Sale share ONE invoice sequence.

Examples:

- INV-0001 Counter
- INV-0002 Online
- INV-0003 OFC
- INV-0004 Counter

Invoice number generation must be concurrency-safe.

Use:
- Postgres sequence, or
- locked counter table

Generate invoice number inside the same transaction that creates the invoice.

Never generate invoice number in the client.

#### Payment Allocations

`payment_allocations` links receipts/payments to invoices, purchases, vendors, customers, riders, or salesmen.

This table is required for:

- partial payment tracking
- receivables
- payables
- salesman commission
- rider COD settlement
- duplicate prevention

Salesman commission must be calculated from collected/allocated payment amounts, not raw invoice totals.

Commission calculation must be idempotent.

Re-processing the same payment must never duplicate commission.

Use unique constraints where needed, such as allocation id + salesman id.

### 2. Sales Module

- Counter Sale
- Online Sale
- OFC / Out-of-City Sale

All share one invoice serial sequence.

#### Counter Sale

- customer optional
- salesman required
- multiple items
- temporary item allowed
- negative stock allowed
- multiple payment methods allowed
- partial payment allowed
- change/refund through different account allowed

Required example:

Bill = Rs. 8,000  
Customer pays Rs. 10,000 cash  
Rs. 2,000 change returned via JazzCash

System must post correctly:

- Cash received: 10,000
- JazzCash paid out: 2,000
- Sales recognized: 8,000

Balanced voucher, not a fudged single line.

#### Salesman Commission

- default commission = 5%
- override allowed per salesman
- commission accrues only after payment collection
- commission based on `payment_allocations`
- no duplicate commission on re-processing
- sales return does not reverse already-accrued commission unless explicitly enabled later

#### Sales Returns

Build as a real minimal module, not a stub.

Return must:

- reverse/adjust original sale
- update stock
- post reversing/adjustment voucher
- keep audit trail
- not reverse already-accrued commission by default

### 3. Online Sale / COD

Online sale is mostly COD.

Flow:

- create order
- customer name/phone/address required
- items
- rider assignment
- statuses:
  - Pending
  - Assigned
  - Out for Delivery
  - Delivered
  - Returned
- rider collects COD
- rider submits COD later/daily

Rider ledger tracks:

- COD assigned
- COD delivered
- COD submitted
- COD pending
- delivery charges

Critical delivery charge rule:

Rider delivery charge belongs to rider unless configured otherwise.

Customer may pay product amount + delivery charge together to rider.

Posting must split:

- business sale revenue = product amount only
- rider earning/settlement = delivery charge

Never merge rider delivery charge into company sales revenue.

### 4. OFC / Out-of-City Sale

- items
- advance received account
- courier/vendor
- delivery/courier charge
- dispatch status

Customer delivery charge and courier actual cost must remain separate.

Courier actual cost is recorded as real expense/vendor payable/payment.

If customer delivery charge is higher than courier cost, profit is visible in reports.

Do not net them together at entry time.

## ANTI-PATTERNS — NEVER DO THESE

- Do not enforce role access only in UI.
- Do not bypass RLS for convenience.
- Do not expose service-role keys to browser.
- Do not build `business_accounts` disconnected from `accounts`.
- Do not give vendors/customers/salesmen/riders manual balance fields as source of truth.
- Do not store opening balances as display-only numbers.
- Do not create standalone `users` table duplicating Supabase Auth.
- Do not use raw JS floating-point math for money.
- Do not use float/double for money columns.
- Do not attempt voucher balancing with row-level CHECK constraint.
- Do not allow direct client inserts into `voucher_lines`.
- Do not generate invoice numbers in the client.
- Do not give each sale type separate invoice counter.
- Do not block sales due to insufficient stock.
- Do not merge rider delivery charges into company sales revenue.
- Do not calculate salesman commission at invoice time.
- Do not calculate commission from raw invoice totals.
- Do not allow duplicate commission from re-processed payments.
- Do not hard-delete posted invoices/vouchers.
- Do not silently adjust cash short/extra.
- Do not show final profit where COGS is still estimated.
- Do not build offline accounting writes in MVP.
- Do not build multi-tenant UI in MVP.
- Do not skip `business_id`.
- Do not batch phases.
- Do not claim “done” without browser verification.
