- COD submitted

==================================================
PART B — CANONICAL ONLINE COD ACCOUNTING POLICY
==================================================

Use this accounting policy unless the existing verified architecture requires an equivalent balanced treatment.

ONLINE ORDER CREATED

The sale invoice already recognizes the product sale.

For a COD order:

- Product amount is posted as business Sales
- Product amount becomes Customer Receivable until delivery
- Stock-out occurs through the existing sale posting
- Rider-owned delivery fee is not credited to company Sales

Example:

Product amount:
- Rs 8,000

Delivery charge:
- Rs 300

Default rider earning:
- Rs 300

At Online COD order creation:

- Customer Receivable debit Rs 8,000
- Sales credit Rs 8,000

The operational customer collection amount is:

- Product Rs 8,000
- Delivery Rs 300
- Total COD Rs 8,300

Do not post Sales revenue twice at delivery.

==================================================
PART C — DELIVERED ORDER ACCOUNTING
==================================================

When the rider marks the order Delivered and confirms collection:

Example:

- Product amount: Rs 8,000
- Customer delivery charge: Rs 300
- Rider earning: Rs 300
- Total collected: Rs 8,300

Expected voucher:

- Rider COD Receivable debit Rs 8,300
- Customer Receivable credit Rs 8,000
- Rider Payable credit Rs 300

Balanced:

- Debit Rs 8,300
- Credit Rs 8,300

Meaning:

- Customer no longer owes the business
- Rider now holds Rs 8,300
- Business owes the rider Rs 300 delivery earning
- Business Sales remains Rs 8,000 only

==================================================
COMPANY SHARE OF DELIVERY CHARGE
==================================================

Allow an authorized configuration where the customer delivery charge is split.

Example:

- Product amount: Rs 8,000
- Customer delivery charge: Rs 300
- Rider earning: Rs 200
- Company delivery income: Rs 100
- Total rider collection: Rs 8,300

Expected delivery voucher:

- Rider COD Receivable debit Rs 8,300
- Customer Receivable credit Rs 8,000
- Rider Payable credit Rs 200
- Delivery Income credit Rs 100

Default rider earning should equal the full delivery charge unless configured otherwise.

Do not credit the company with rider-owned delivery income.

==================================================
PART D — COD SUBMISSION ACCOUNTING
==================================================

Support two settlement methods.

METHOD 1 — RIDER SUBMITS FULL/GROSS COD

Example:

Rider submits:
- Rs 8,300

Expected:

- Cash/Bank debit Rs 8,300
- Rider COD Receivable credit Rs 8,300

Rider Payable remains Rs 300.

Later rider earning payment:

- Rider Payable debit Rs 300
- Cash/Bank credit Rs 300

METHOD 2 — RIDER RETAINS DELIVERY FEE AND SUBMITS NET COD

Example:

- Rider COD held: Rs 8,300
- Rider earning: Rs 300
- Rider submits cash: Rs 8,000

Expected settlement voucher:

- Cash/Bank debit Rs 8,000
- Rider Payable debit Rs 300
- Rider COD Receivable credit Rs 8,300

Balanced.

The rider payable is settled through deduction.

==================================================
PARTIAL COD SUBMISSION
==================================================

Allow partial COD submission.

Example:

Rider COD pending:
- Rs 20,000

Rider submits:
- Rs 12,000

Expected:

- Cash/Bank debit Rs 12,000
- Rider COD Receivable credit Rs 12,000
- Remaining COD pending Rs 8,000

Rules:

- Cannot submit more than COD pending plus any explicitly supported settlement adjustment
- Cannot allocate the same delivered order amount twice
- Partial settlement must update rider/order allocation correctly
- Accounting and operational balances must agree
