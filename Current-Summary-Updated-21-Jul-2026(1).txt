7. CLIENT UAT — CHANGE REQUEST BATCH 1
======================================================================

This batch was received after the client tested the deployed ERP.
The following business/accounting decisions are now locked.

A. HOME / BUSINESS SUMMARY DATE RANGE
- Home must support direct period selection.
- User should be able to view Today, Last 3 Days, Last 7 Days, This Month or a Custom Range.
- The selected period must consistently control period-based business-summary figures.
- Point-in-time balances must remain clearly separate from period activity.

B. SALE, RETURN AND NET DAILY ACTIVITY
- Counter, Online, OFC and Other Sale use the same sale/return principles.
- A return from an older bill can be entered on today's bill as a negative line.
- Every negative line must reference the original invoice and original sale line.
- The original posted bill must remain immutable.
- The system should create the required linked return/audit record and accounting reversal underneath, while the current bill/screen shows positive sale lines and negative return lines together.
- Example:
  - Yesterday: 5 pieces sold.
  - Today: 5 new pieces sold and 2 pieces from yesterday returned.
  - Today's activity shows Sale +5, Return -2, Net +3.
- Stock, customer balance, revenue, COGS and commission must reverse exactly once for the returned quantity.

C. OTHER SALE
- Add a fourth sale category named "Other Sale".
- It is a customer-account/credit sale for cases where the customer takes goods now and pays later.
- Customer selection is mandatory.
- Paid amount may be zero or partial.
- The unpaid amount creates/updates customer receivable.
- Later receipts must be linked to the customer and preferably to the specific Other Sale bill.
- Other Sale follows the same return, commission, identity-number and reporting rules as other sale types.

D. PRODUCT-WISE COMMISSION — LOCKED FLOW
- Commission rate is configured per product in whole rupees per piece, e.g. Rs 20.
- Commission is calculated and accrued when a sale/return bill is posted.
- Actual payment to Owner/Salesman happens later through a separate settlement/payment entry.
- A sale adds commission; a return creates a commission reversal.
- Net commissionable quantity for the relevant seller/product = sold quantity - returned quantity.
- Example:
  - Today sold 5 at Rs 20 = +Rs 100 commission.
  - Today returned 2 from an older bill = -Rs 40 commission.
  - Net commission movement today = Rs 60.
- Return commission must reverse against the seller recorded on the original sale line, even if another user records the return today.
- Applies to Counter, Online, OFC and Other Sale.
- Every commission movement must show:
  - separate identity/reference
  - current bill reference
  - original bill reference for returns
  - seller/Owner
  - product
  - sold quantity
  - returned quantity
  - net quantity
  - per-piece rate
  - positive or negative commission movement
  - running payable balance
- No duplicate commission posting is allowed.

E. OWNER COMMISSION — REAL EXPENSE
- If Owner personally makes a sale, commission belongs to Owner.
- It is a real business commission expense as requested by the client.
- Recommended posting:
  - Commission Expense Dr
  - Owner Commission Payable Cr
- When paid:
  - Owner Commission Payable Dr
  - Cash/Bank/Petty Cash Cr
- Keep Owner commission separately identifiable from drawings.
- Accountant should confirm local tax treatment outside the software design.

F. ONLINE SALE RIDER ASSIGNMENT
- Online Sale must include rider assignment.
- One complete order is assigned to one rider.
- COD amount is tied to that rider/order.
- Owner/Admin/Accountant can manage delivery status where permitted.
- Rider can update status from the rider dashboard.
- All status changes synchronize immediately and record actor, time and source.

G. RIDER DELIVERY, PARTIAL RETURN AND SETTLEMENT — LOCKED ACCOUNTING FLOW
- Rider UI must be extremely simple: assigned order plus large clear action buttons.
- Required actions:
  - Deliver
  - Partial Delivery
  - Return
  - Partial Return
- Example:
  - ordered 2
  - delivered 1
  - returned 1
- On delivery/COD collection:
  - Rider COD Receivable / Cash with Rider Dr
  - Customer Receivable Cr
- On rider settlement:
  - Cash/Bank/Petty Cash Dr
  - Rider COD Receivable / Cash with Rider Cr
- Return/partial return reverses sale, stock, customer receivable and rider expected COD for only the returned quantity.
- Owner/Accountant settlement screen and Rider dashboard must show the same live status.
- Settlement view must show:
  - total assigned parcels
  - delivered parcels
  - returned/partial-return parcels
  - unsettled parcels
  - COD expected
  - COD received from rider
  - rider-held/outstanding amount
- Settlement must be auditable and linked to orders, status actions and receipt/accounting entries.

H. IDENTITY NUMBER FOR EVERY ENTRY
- Every transaction/business entry must have a readable identity number.
- Example: Purchase PUR-0001.
- Audit all existing entry types and add missing sequences without breaking the shared sale invoice sequence.
- Prefixes and reset rules must be documented and consistent.

I. ACCOUNT CATEGORY SUBCATEGORIES
- Account Categories need parent/subcategory support.
- Reports and Chart of Accounts must continue grouping correctly.
- Use a simple expandable hierarchy and avoid unnecessary deep nesting.

J. EXPANDED EASY CONTRA / MONEY MOVEMENT — LOCKED FLOW
- Use one simple "Contra & Transfers" screen for daily money movement.
- The UI may contain multiple expandable rows and one save action.
- Each row must select a transaction type:
  1. Internal Transfer
     - Destination Cash/Bank/Petty Cash Dr
     - Source Cash/Bank/Petty Cash Cr
     - no income/expense effect
  2. Owner Drawing
     - Drawings Dr

  3. Capital Introduced

- Do not post Owner Drawing as a fake cash-to-cash Contra.

APPROVED BUSINESS DECISIONS
======================================================================

1. COMMISSION TRIGGER
- Commission is calculated from net eligible quantity:
  sold quantity minus returned quantity.
- Commission is calculated when the bill is posted.
- It becomes earned/payable proportionally when customer payment is collected.
- Returns reduce or reverse the related commission exactly once.
- For Online COD, collection is recognized for commission when rider settlement is completed and the business receives/settles the COD amount.
- Example:
  10 sold, 2 returned, rate Rs 20 = Rs 160 eligible commission.
  If 50% eligible payment is collected, Rs 80 becomes payable initially.

2. RIDER-HELD COD
- COD collected from customers remains associated with the assigned rider until settlement.
- It does not need to appear as a normal user-managed Cash or Bank account.
- Backend accounting must still remain balanced through a protected system-controlled account such as:
  Rider Held COD / Cash with Rider.
- The system account must not be editable or deletable by normal users.
- It may remain hidden from ordinary account-entry screens.
- It must be included correctly in Balance Sheet reporting as a Current Asset.
- Rider Settlement UI should show:
  COD collected,
  amount received from rider,
  and remaining amount held by rider.
- On settlement, the amount moves from Rider Held COD to the selected Cash/Bank account.
- Partial delivery and returns must recognize only the actual collectible/collected COD.
- Settlement and reversal must be idempotent and auditable.
